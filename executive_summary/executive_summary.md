# Executive Summary — Hotel Revenue Management Case Study

## Project Overview

This project presents an end-to-end Hotel Revenue Management case study based on hotel booking data.

The objective is to demonstrate how booking data can be transformed into practical business insights related to segmentation, cancellation risk, demand forecasting, pricing behavior and revenue strategy.

The project is structured as a complete Revenue Management analysis, moving from data exploration to strategic recommendations.

---

## Business Problem

Hotels need to make pricing, inventory and commercial decisions under uncertainty.

Demand can change depending on season, customer segment, booking channel and lead time. At the same time, cancellations and price sensitivity can affect the final revenue captured by the hotel.

This project addresses the following business question:

**How can hotel booking data be used to support better Revenue Management decisions?**

---

## Analysis Modules

### 1. Segment Performance Analysis

This module evaluates how different customer segments contribute to room nights, revenue and ADR.

The purpose is to identify which segments provide stronger revenue value and which segments may generate volume without proportional revenue performance.

---

### 2. Cancellation Risk Analysis

This module analyzes cancellation behavior and its impact on revenue performance.

The purpose is to understand how cancellations affect net revenue, forecast accuracy, inventory availability and potential overbooking decisions.

---

### 3. Demand Forecasting by Segment and Month

This module forecasts demand across months and customer segments.

The purpose is to support pricing and inventory decisions by anticipating demand patterns and seasonal behavior.

---

### 4. Price Elasticity and Demand Behavior

This module analyzes the relationship between ADR and demand.

The purpose is to evaluate how demand reacts to price changes and how pricing decisions should be interpreted depending on seasonality and segment behavior.

---

### 5. Revenue Management Strategy Recommendations

This final module translates analytical findings into practical Revenue Management recommendations.

The focus is on pricing strategy, segment prioritization, cancellation risk management, channel mix and inventory protection.

---

## Key Revenue Management Insights

The main insights from the case study are:

- Revenue performance should not be evaluated only by booking volume or occupancy.
- Segment mix is critical because some segments may generate stronger revenue value than others.
- Cancellation behavior affects net demand, forecast accuracy and inventory control.
- Demand forecasting should be analyzed by month and segment, not only at total hotel level.
- Price elasticity must be interpreted carefully because ADR often reacts to demand pressure.
- Inventory should be protected during high-demand periods for higher-value demand.
- Technical analysis becomes more valuable when translated into clear business actions.

---

## Main Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the main Revenue Management recommendations are:

1. Evaluate segments using room nights, revenue and ADR together.
2. Prioritize stronger-value segments during high-demand periods.
3. Monitor cancellation risk by channel and segment.
4. Use cancellation patterns to improve net demand forecasts.
5. Forecast demand by month and segment to support pricing decisions.
6. Avoid applying one single pricing rule to all segments.
7. Interpret price elasticity together with seasonality and demand pressure.
8. Protect inventory from low-yield or high-risk bookings during strong demand periods.
9. Connect data analysis with practical commercial actions.
10. Use the findings as decision support, not as automatic pricing rules.
